## Overview

The Bloodbound Legion is the primary faith devoted to [[Rodikar]], and it reflects the god’s uncompromising view that power must be earned through conflict. Unlike churches built around comfort, charity, or quiet contemplation, the Legion is built around discipline, readiness, and proof. To belong is to train. To lead is to win. To preach is to demonstrate.

The Legion occupies an uneasy place in [[Faysel]]. In some regions, its members are respected as defenders, generals, and disciplined guardians who ensure that threats are met with decisive force. In others, they are feared as conquerors and aggressors who see opportunity in every border and weakness in every hesitation. Rodikar does not distinguish between protection and conquest. Both are tests of strength. Both are means of shaping the world.

Its institutional heart lies at [[Sento Monastery]], where doctrine and discipline intertwine. Yet the Legion is not monolithic. Different sects interpret Rodikar’s will through local culture. Some emphasize defense and honorable warfare. Others glorify expansion and domination. What unites them is not uniform morality, but a shared conviction that conflict is the crucible through which worth is proven.

Politically, the Bloodbound Legion rarely seeks subtle influence. It does not embed itself quietly in courts or guilds. Instead, it earns standing through battlefield results, tournament victories, and the reputation of its champions. Rulers tolerate or court the Legion not because it is gentle, but because it is effective.

## Culture

### Dogma 

The Bloodbound Legion teaches that conflict is not an aberration in the world, but its refining force. War reveals truth. Competition exposes weakness. Victory establishes order. Rodikar’s followers believe that strength, skill, and resolve are the only reliable foundations of authority. Peace without the capacity for violence is fragile. Law without the will to enforce it is hollow.

Devotees are expected to pursue mastery of their chosen form of combat, whether as soldiers, duelists, tacticians, or commanders. They are taught to respect worthy opponents and to despise cowardice more than defeat. Bloodshed is celebrated for any sake, and acknowledged as the cost of decisive change. A victory earned through discipline and open contest is sacred. A triumph achieved through hidden treachery is suspect.

Rodikar's followers believe in the glory of battle and the pursuit of martial excellence. They see conflict as a means of testing one's mettle. His followers understand that conflict shapes nations and civilizations. Rodikar teaches that war is an inevitable force in the world, and those who master it can shape their destinies. He emphasizes strategy and the pursuit of victory above all else. His followers are expected to wage war with unwavering determination when conflict arises. He revels in competition, where only the strongest prevail. His followers are encouraged to embrace the chaos of war, seeking victory and understanding that bloodshed is the price of power.

> <u>**Tenets:**</u>
> - **Peace in the Violence:** Followers of Rodikar embrace the crucible of combat as the ultimate test of strength and skill, believing that adversity and conflict forge might and valor.
> 
> - **Martial Prowess:** Strive for excellence in combat and warfare, for it is through strength and skill that civilizations are forged.
> 
> - **Earned Authority:** No one deserves to rule who cannot defend their claim. Power must be proven and maintained.
> 
> - **Honor the Worthy:** Respect those who stand and fight. A fallen foe who met you openly is not to be mocked.
> 
> - **Unrelenting Aggression:** Seek victory. Conquer with purpose and unwavering determination. Ensure the vanquished find their place in the new order.
> 
> - **The Blood Offering:** Spill blood in the name of victory, for it is the currency of power.

### Clergy & Worshippers

The Legion’s clergy are known collectively as the Bloodbound. They are less priests in flowing robes and more captains, instructors, champions, and veterans. Many serve as officers in standing armies, leaders of mercenary companies, arena masters, or martial tutors to nobility. Sermons are often delivered in training yards or war councils rather than in quiet halls.

Rites within the Legion frequently take the form of trials. Duels mark promotion. Strategic exercises determine leadership. New initiates prove their devotion not through recited creed, but through endurance, skill, and willingness to face danger. Those who shrink from challenge do not remain long within its ranks.

Worshippers extend beyond formal soldiers. Gladiators, adventurers, bodyguards, and even ambitious rulers may invoke Rodikar’s name. Some are drawn by the promise of glory. Others by the clarity of a world measured in victory and defeat. Across sects, the common thread is acceptance of struggle as necessary and even sacred.

### Symbols

The primary symbol of the Bloodbound Legion is a blood red trident, most often displayed against a black field. It appears on banners carried into battle, carved into weapon hilts, and etched into the walls of training halls and fortresses. The trident represents decisive force applied with precision, three points converging into a single strike.

Symbolism within the Legion is closely tied to rank and accomplishment. Officers wear distinct markings earned through victory. Scars are not hidden. Weapons are often personalized and ritually blessed before campaigns. Blood offerings, whether symbolic or literal, remain a visible expression of devotion across nearly all sects.

Uniforms vary by region and sect, but martial presentation is consistent. Armor is maintained with care. Standards are carried with pride. Within the Bloodbound Legion, faith is not primarily displayed through ornament, but through readiness and the visible marks of proven conflict.
